Juliette Bertrand

'

I recently joined the Thoth team as a computer vision engineer, collaborating with professor Julien Mairal and Dr. Sophie Giffard-Roisin . Previously, I was a researcher at the Czech Technical University in Prague, member of the VRG group and collaborating with professor Giorgos Tolias and Dr Yannis Kalantidis at Naverlabs Europe . During my time with them, I focused on the challenging task of representation learning and matching for video understanding, particularly when dealing with limited annotated data. Before that, I actively contributed to various applied research projects in the field of machine learning, spanning a wide range of tasks, including re-identifying soccer players through jersey number recognition, real-time ball tracking in low-resolution sports videos, even in the presence of frequent occlusion, predicting the primary topic of webpages with sparse annotated data, forecasting webpage view counts over a 6-month period using historical data, and inferring disparity maps around individuals' faces to match their size and orientation. In 2013, I successfully completed my master's degree at Ecole Centrale Lyon, and I also graduated with distinction in Image and Signal Processing from the University of Lyon.

Outside of work, I enjoy hiking, dancing, acting, traveling, and embracing new cultures. I have a deep interest for cinema and the performing arts, to the extent that I even collaborated with a group of musical aficionados to create a musical entirely from scratch.
